# ITC Limited — 3-Statement Revenue Projection Model (FY21–FY28E)

> **A bottom-up financial model built from ITC's public annual reports, projecting segment-level revenue, margins, and key ratios through FY28.**

---

## 📌 Project Summary

ITC Limited is one of India's most complex conglomerates — five entirely different businesses (cigarettes, FMCG foods, hotels, agribusiness, paperboards) operating under one balance sheet. A simple top-line revenue number tells you almost nothing.

This model breaks ITC apart segment by segment, builds projections from the ground up using defensible assumptions, and surfaces the "so what" behind each number — the way a consultant would approach it.

---

## 🗂️ Model Structure

| Sheet | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Cover** | Project metadata, analyst info, disclaimer |
| **Historical** | Segment revenue, P&L (EBITDA / EBIT / PAT), balance sheet highlights — FY21 to FY25 |
| **Assumptions** | Dynamic input table — growth rates per segment, margin assumptions, balance sheet growth (all editable) |
| **Projections** | FY26E–FY28E forecasts linked to assumptions; full P&L + balance sheet + ROCE |
| **Ratios** | OPM%, PAT Margin%, ROCE%, Asset Turnover — FY21 through FY28E in one view |
| **Commentary** | One-line strategic thesis per segment |

## 🔮 Projection Assumptions (FY26E–FY28E)

| Segment | FY26E Growth | FY27E Growth | FY28E Growth | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FMCG — Cigarettes | 5.6% | 5.0% | 4.5% | Pricing-led; volume maturity |
| FMCG — Others | 9.0% | 8.5% | 8.0% | YiPPee!, Bingo & new launches scaling |
| Hotels | 12.0% | 10.0% | 9.0% | Post-COVID recovery + Mövenpick pipeline |
| Agribusiness | 4.0% | 4.5% | 5.0% | Commodity-linked; modest recovery |
| Paperboards & Packaging | 5.0% | 5.5% | 6.0% | E-commerce packaging tailwind |

**Margin assumptions:** OPM stable at ~36.5–37.0%; PAT Margin ~31.5–32.0%

---

## 💡 Strategic Insights

- **Cigarettes (54% of revenue)** — Cash cow with exceptional pricing power. Volume growth slowing but margins hold. The business that funds everything else.
- **FMCG Others** — ITC's growth engine. Scaling fast but still only ~11% of revenue. The re-rating thesis depends on this segment reaching ₹15,000 Cr+ by FY28.
- **Hotels** — Real post-COVID recovery underway; small in scale but high strategic visibility.
- **Agribusiness** — Commodity-linked and volatile. FY24 export policy headwinds exposed the structural ceiling.
- **Paperboards & Packaging** — Structural tailwind from e-commerce. Next capex cycle will determine if this segment earns its cost of capital.
